What's happening in Xinjiang?

As heightened intersectional activism takes center stage globally and on social media, new human rights issues have come to light. However, misinformation and a lack of reliable sources have impeded some of these issues from emerging. Chief among these include the Uighur Muslim internment camps in the Xinjiang province of China, which came to international attention as early as 2018 but had little public outcry until recently. Let’s explore the subject further and address what has been done to combat the persecution.

Tensions and violence between the Uighur Muslims, ethnic minorities of Xinjiang, and the Chinese government have been on the rise due to the latter’s historical exploitation of the former. The first indication of escalation were satellite images provided by Google Earth and the European Space Agency. They depicted the silent construction of 268 internment facilities with watchtowers, fences, and buildings rising up where soccer fields, schools, and desert used to be. The largest is estimated to hold at least 130,000 among the one million imprisoned Uighurs. Furthermore, many Muslims faced privacy violations as well as discriminatory policies like banning Islamic practices, and Uighur Muslims living outside the country have reported their family members in Xinjiang have abruptly gone missing. One overseas Uighur Muslim told BBC that “there is no-one left outside” the camps. 

Recent testimonials from Uighur prisoners released during the earlier stages of the confinement process describe dehumanizing conditions, including frequent beatings, brainwashing, forced sterilization, and slave labor. Yet, China’s government categorically denies any wrongdoing, portraying the camps as re-education schools to prevent terrorism and religious extremism and dismissing activists’ claims as “outright nonsense”. This stance provoked an international backlash from countries and activist groups, with the United States imposing sanctions to hold the government accountable and human rights groups pushing for the United Nations to get involved. However, some activists have been comparing the scale of the Uighur internment camps to the Holocaust, which is not only statistically inaccurate but deeply insensitive to the Jewish community. While it is paramount to highlight this blatant abuse of human rights, we should not compare genocides to validate them. 

Finally, it is essential to recognize the positive and negative consequences of technology on this subject. Publicly available satellite images allowed for the human rights issue to be discovered and then globally circulated in the first place. On the contrary, facial recognition and devices designed to collect data and images from phones enabled the Chinese government to profile and subsequently intern Uighur Muslims. Social media promoted publicity of the issue, yet it also generated hurtful and false comparisons. Throughout history and even now, STEM has been a double-edged sword, and this topic only exemplifies that those who wield it should be educated and ethical.

 
 
 
 
 
 

Snoe Castillo

As STEMchats’ Marketing Lead, Snoe Castillo works to shape STEMchat’s social presence while working towards her aspirations at the intersection of STEM and social justice. Castillo was prepared for a medical career at Dozier-Libbey Medical High School and continues working towards her mission as a Film major and Humanities minor at San Jose State University. 

Castillo shares that working as Marketing Lead is anything but an independent endeavor. “My primary roles include managing our social media presence, creating graphics, and essentially assisting the programs with any promotional needs. However, our Marketing team now consists of 8 people, so we work together to make a cohesive and educational social media experience for our audiences,” shared Castillo. 

In addition to being a current Film major, Castillo learned in a STEM-centric environment during high school. “I attended a medical pathway high school, which meant that the school’s curriculum would prepare us for future medical careers,” explained Castillo. “I got to create many projects on medical inequities and how we, as a future generation can remedy and stay aware of these topics. I also participated in a 7-hour math competition in my last year of high school with a group of my peers.”

Castillo’s STEM experiences inform an appreciation for STEM and how it intersects with social justice, such as in the mission of STEMchats. “I think STEM is so incredibly interesting, and even though I’m not majoring in it, I still enjoy the knowledge it entails,” said Castillo.  “A lot of people don’t realize that STEM and Social Justice actually intertwine a lot. There’s climate change and how it disproportionately affects impoverished POC [Person of Color] communities as well as scientific experiments, specifically in the US, that have targeted BIPOC [Black, Indigenous Persons of Color]. I enjoy educating people on these topics because of the fact that we simply need to care about one another and providing knowledge nurtures that care.”

Both with and beyond STEMchats, Castillo works towards issues she is passionate about. “My goal in life is to create representation for minorities that never had that. I remember when I was younger, I first didn’t even think I was Filipino since I didn’t know what that meant,” shared Castillo. “I never saw any Filipino representation on screen or paper …  It’s so vital to see someone like yourself, whether it’s gender orientation, sexual orientation, race, or even disability, and I truly want to be able to provide that for future generations.” 

Looking forward to the future, Castillo looks to graduate with a Bachelor’s degree and continue her work with STEMchats. “Within STEMchats I’m really hoping to create resources that are accessible to all and that help students understand STEM topics.”

 
 

Grasshopper Ears and their Unique Function                                                               Rather than having ears on the sides of their head as humans do, grasshoppers’ ears are located on the sides of their abdomen. This fascinating insect’s sound detection organ is called a tympanum, a thin membrane with similar characteristics to a drum. If the tympanum detects a sound from its surroundings, it will elicit a vibration, just like when a mallet hits a drum with the same frequency and amplitude, and the drum responds with a similar vibration. This reciprocation of vibrations is commonly used to communicate with mating partners and protect a grasshopper’s territory.

Wildfires and the Carbon Cycle                                                                           Throughout the past half-century, carbon dioxide levels have been increasing in the atmosphere, directly linked with the expansive effects of global warming. Forest ecosystems play a large role in housing carbon, both in leaves and organic material. However, as deforestation and the burning of forests persists through wildfires, this can lead to tremendous contributions of atmospheric carbon dioxide. The stored carbon becomes released into the atmosphere, inhibiting natural vegetation and the overall pattern of the carbon cycle. This then leads to an increase in climate change and tremendous environmental consequences for the future.

A Teaspoon of a Neutron Star Weighs Around a Billion Tons                                              A neutron star is a dense collection of neutrons that forms as a result of a massive star explosion. A tablespoon of a neutron star would weigh about 1 billion tons, which is about the weight of Mount Everest. If this were placed on Earth, there wouldn’t be any significant effects of local gravity relative to a person standing next to it. However, this small piece of a heavy neutron star will begin expanding and possibly turn into an explosion that could lead to several consequences.

 

Welcome to Sci-Fi or Fact, the newest installation to our newsletter where we highlight the intersection between STEM and pop culture! In honor of Chadwick Boseman and his work as the Black Panther, we’ve decided to delve into the science of his suit, specifically its material, Vibranium. How does it work? And is it possible in real life? Let’s find out!

In the Marvel universe, Vibranium is an ore, dropped on Earth by a meteor, from the fictional nation of Wakanda. It’s able to absorb all vibrations (hence the name) upon impact, making it extremely flexible yet sturdy. Because of these highly advanced technological properties, Vibranium is a key material within the weapons and armor of many superheroes, including Captain America’s shield and, of course, Black Panther’s suit. As seen in both the comics and the movies, Captain America’s shield is practically impenetrable since it’s made of a steel-Vibranium alloy. Similarly, the Vibranium within Black Panther’s suit protects him from incoming projectiles while also allowing for agile movement. 

Now, is such a material possible in real life? According to the Law of Conservation of Energy, energy cannot be created or destroyed. If Vibranium did exist in real life, where would the absorbed vibrational energy go? According to Professor James Kakalios, author of The Physics of Superheroes, the vibrational energy could potentially be turned into light through a process called sonoluminescence. Referencing a scene from The Avengers where a bright flash of light emits after Thor’s hammer hits Captain America’s Vibranium shield, Kakalios indicated that, theoretically, through sonoluminescence, we could create a semblance of Vibranium that would follow the laws of physics. 

Unfortunately, we can’t quite yet put this technology into a weapon or armor from the existing materials currently available to us. However, we do have a close replica called Graphene. Made of strongly bonded carbon atoms, Graphene is a super thin and durable material. Similar to Vibranium, Graphene is able to dilute energy from projectiles, preventing the bonds between the material from breaking… although the technology is still far from perfect. 

However, regardless of whether we can recreate this fictional ore or not, this material has definitely sparked a long conversation about the junction between science and superheroes. Thank you, Chadwick Boseman, for originating such an impactful role and for also stimulating a long-lasting conversation on technological innovation.
