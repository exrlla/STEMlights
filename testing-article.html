Godzilla vs. Kong

Image from Kirainet

Today, we will be debunking the science behind the famous film, “Godzilla vs. Kong”. To recap, this movie is essentially about a titan-gorilla, Kong, and a titan-dinosaur, Godzilla, clashing to the death multiple times to establish titan supremacy. However, the plot of the movie also revolves around a tech organization known as Apex Cybernetics. This company claimed to work towards advancing the world of technology, but Walter Simmons, the founder of Apex, had the true intention of eliminating titans from existence. He planned on accomplishing this by creating a mechanized titan known as Mechagodzilla. The interesting part was that this mechanism was entirely controlled through the mind! Is that even possible? Can robots really be controlled telepathically? Let’s find out!

Well, during a project conducted by a team from Boston University (BU) and MIT’s Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL), researchers found a way to control robots using their minds. During this experiment, these teams monitored signals within the brain through a device known as an electroencephalography (EEG) monitor. Using this device, researchers were able to detect error-related potential (ErrP) signals which are signals the brain automatically created when a human detects some sort of error. With the EEG monitor detecting ErrP signals, this team developed a robot that recognized mistakes almost instantaneously. To view this amazing robot click here. Specifically, this robot was designed to organize items in two categories. Once the robot detected any ErrP signals, the robot would correct its course of action. If an incorrect action has already been done, the robot was even able to display embarrassment. Overall, this experiment proves that mind controlled robots are possible today, but can humans control robots to the extent that Apex Cybernetics did with Mechagodzilla?

During an advanced experiment of the ErrP detection project, the same CSAIL and BU team found a way to correlate muscle actions with robots. These researchers used a biosignal known as the Electromyography (EMG) signal to correct robot actions more precisely. EMG is also used to control prosthetic limbs. These biosignals are emitted whenever any actions of movement are done. The robot used to conduct this experiment utilized both EEG and EMG signals to drill one of three targets. If a mistake was made, researchers focused on movement of the arm to manually direct the drill in the right direction. To view this experiment click here. In the end, this experiment showed that humans are able to use bodily movements to guide robots’ actions.

In “Godzilla vs Kong”, a person controlling Mechagodzilla neurally links and transfers their consciousness to the machine. Although it is proven that neurally linking a person to a robot to guide their actions is possible, the extent to which Apex Cybernetics controlled Mechagodizilla may be a little out of reach. Today, engineers are only able to accomplish simple tasks such as categorizing items and drilling products. To conclude, the advanced technology seen in Mechagodzilla from “Godzilla vs Kong” is not impossible, but the complex movement used to attack the two titans may take some time to accomplish. 

 
 
 
 

Spain’s Mar Menor Turns Green

What used to be a sparkling blue lagoon in Spain is now covered with algal blooms and dead fish. This change comes from the increasing threat of climate change and runoff from nearby farms. Rainfall causes the nitrate fertilizers to flow into the lagoon, drastically increasing the nitrate levels. This process is called eutrophication and it causes algae to bloom more, creating a layer on the water’s surface. The algae blocks off sunlight, making it much more difficult for photosynthesis to occur. The algae and plants eventually decompose increasing carbon dioxide levels and making the water more acidic. Fish and other organisms struggle to find food and breathe, leading to them dying as the ecosystem experiences ruination.

 

Planet MOA-2010-BLG-477b

Astronomers recently discovered a planet the size of Jupiter orbiting a white dwarf 6,500 light years from Earth. A white dwarf is when a star uses up all of its nuclear fuel, turning white but maintaining its hot core. This is a revolutionary discovery as the planet is surviving even after its star has died. Scientist Lisa Kaltenegger from the Carl Sagan Institute at Cornell University, believes that this planet could provide more information about Earth’s future when the sun dies. She believes a “second genesis” will occur, creating new organisms.

Pig Kidneys for Humans

Could pigs be the solution for future human organ transplants? Surgeons at N.Y.U. Langone Health attached a pig kidney, that was grown in a genetically modified pig, to a human patient. This is a new approach to xenotransplantation, a transplant of living cells, tissue, and organs, that goes from one species to another. The kidney was attached to the patient’s leg blood vessels to test if the organ would function with the human body. The kidney was successful outside of the body and even worked immediately, unlike most kidney transplants. There are currently over 100,000 in need of an organ transplant, with 90,240 of those in need of kidneys.

The Creation of Element 118

Photo from Dmarcus100

In 2002, a group of researchers at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research at Dubna synthesized oganesson (element 118) by blasting californium atoms with calcium ions for approximately 1,080 hours. This reaction produced a single atom of oganesson. However, little is known about element 118. It is particularly difficult to study due to its impracticality and the fact that ​​it decays instantaneously (after a mere millisecond).

In 2018, the discovery of the 118th element was officially recognized by the International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry (IUPAC) as well as the International Union of Pure and Applied Physics (IUPAP). The element is named after Yuri Oganessian, deemed the the world's leading researcher in superheavy chemical elements thanks to his development of the “hot fusion” and “cold fusion” methods (key to their discovery). He is the only living person to have an element named after him. 

Superheavy elements are generally defined as elements with atomic numbers that are greater than 100, although there is no agreed upon definition in the science world. Because this element is so difficult to produce, oganesson has no uses outside of scientific research (at least right now). Though the element is positioned with the noble gases, many believe that, “it is neither a gas nor “noble”—that is, unreactive.” Element 118 currently has the highest atomic number and highest atomic mass in the entire periodic table. It is also one of the most recently discovered, alongside 113, 115, 116, 117. 

Element 119 is still purely hypothetical, but scientists are eager to learn more. Many have already fleshed out theories and predictions in anticipation of that exciting discovery!

You Are What You Eat? 

The food that we put into our mouths have long lasting impacts on our bodies — including our brains! For example, our brain is full of fatty acids that create and maintain or cell membranes, protecting us from many diseases. That is why you're often encouraged to eat omega-rich foods. However, the consumption of trans or saturated fat may cause poor brain health. That is why it is important to learn about a healthy, balanced diet; take care of your body, especially your brain!
